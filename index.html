Dilution: The Core Principle

Aim & Principle

In laboratories, it is common to prepare a dilute solution from a more concentrated standard solution (a stock solution). This process is called dilution.

The Golden Rule: During dilution, only solvent (usually distilled water) is added. Therefore, the number of moles of solute remains constant.

Because Concentration (Molarity) is defined as:

$$ Molarity (M) = \frac{\text{Moles of solute } (n)}{\text{Volume of solution } (V)} $$

It follows that \( n = M \times V \). Since \( n \) is constant before and after adding water, we arrive at the fundamental dilution formula.

The Dilution Equation

$$ M_1 V_1 = M_2 V_2 $$
  • \( M_1 \) = Initial molarity (concentration of stock solution)
  • \( V_1 \) = Initial volume (volume of stock solution taken)
  • \( M_2 \) = Final molarity (concentration of diluted solution)
  • \( V_2 \) = Final total volume (stock volume + water added)

Pro-Tip for HKDSE:

The volumes \( V_1 \) and \( V_2 \) can be in \( \text{cm}^3 \) or \( \text{dm}^3 \), as long as you use the same unit for both sides of the equation! However, to calculate actual moles \( (n) \), volume must be in \( \text{dm}^3 \).

Laboratory Procedure

Standard protocol for precise dilution using volumetric apparatus.

1

Calculate & Transfer

Calculate the required volume of stock solution using \(M_1V_1 = M_2V_2\).

Use a previously rinsed volumetric pipette (and pipette filler) to precisely transfer this volume into a clean volumetric flask.

Safety: Never pipette by mouth. Always use a pipette filler.
2

Initial Dilution

Add distilled water (or deionized water) to the volumetric flask using a wash bottle until the bulb of the flask is about two-thirds full.

Swirl the flask gently to mix the contents.

Note: If diluting concentrated sulphuric acid, add the acid slowly to a large volume of water while stirring, to dissipate the heat evolved.
3

Making up to the Mark

Continue adding distilled water until the liquid level is just below the graduation mark on the neck of the flask.

Use a dropper to add distilled water drop by drop until the bottom of the meniscus aligns exactly with the graduation mark when viewed at eye level.

4

Final Mixing

Stopper the volumetric flask tightly.

Invert the flask multiple times (at least 10 times) to ensure the solution is thoroughly and uniformly mixed.

Common Traps & Misconceptions

The Error
"I should rinse the volumetric flask with the stock solution before transferring it, to ensure accuracy."
Right Concept: Never rinse a volumetric flask with the stock solution! It should only be rinsed with distilled water. Rinsing with stock solution leaves residual solute, which adds extra moles and makes the final concentration incorrectly high.
The Error
"\(V_2\) in the formula \(M_1V_1 = M_2V_2\) represents the volume of water added."
Right Concept: \(V_2\) represents the total final volume of the solution.
\( \text{Volume of water added} = V_2 - V_1 \).
The Error
"When making up to the mark, I should align the top edge of the liquid with the graduation line."
Right Concept: Always align the bottom of the meniscus with the graduation mark (for colorless or lightly colored solutions). Ensure your eye is exactly horizontal to the mark to avoid parallax error.

Revision Summary

Core Principles

  • Dilution is adding solvent (water) to decrease the concentration of a solution.
  • The number of moles of solute remains constant during dilution. (\(n_{\text{initial}} = n_{\text{final}}\))
  • Equation: \(M_1V_1 = M_2V_2\)
  • To find moles, remember to convert \( \text{cm}^3 \) to \( \text{dm}^3 \) by dividing by 1000: \( n = M \times \frac{V(\text{in cm}^3)}{1000} \).

Apparatus & Technique

  • Use a Volumetric Pipette to deliver an exact, fixed volume of the stock solution.
  • Use a Volumetric Flask to contain the final exact total volume of the diluted solution.
  • Rinsing Protocol:
    • Pipette: Rinse with distilled water, then with the stock solution.
    • Volumetric Flask: Rinse with distilled water only.
  • Add water dropwise using a dropper near the graduation mark to ensure the bottom of the meniscus rests exactly on the line.
  • Always stopper and invert the flask to mix.

Wrong Method — Concentration Too High

Pipetted moles: 0.025 mol
Residue moles: ≈ +0.003 mol
Total n ≈ 0.028 mol
M₂ = 0.028 ÷ 0.250 ≈ 0.112 M ✗

Residual solute from the stock-solution rinse dissolved into the final solution, adding extra moles and raising the concentration above the intended 0.100 M.

Correct Method — Exact Concentration

Pipetted moles: 0.025 mol
Residue moles: 0.000 mol
Total n = 0.025 mol
M₂ = 0.025 ÷ 0.250 = 0.100 M ✓

Clean glass means only the pipetted moles are present. The concentration is exactly 0.100 M as designed.
